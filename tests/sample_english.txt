PETITION UNDER ARTICLE 226 OF THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA

MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

1. The Petitioner is a company duly incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013
   bearing CIN U72900MH2015PTC267543. The Petitioner is engaged in the business
   of software development and IT services.

2. By an Assessment Order dated 15th March 2025, the Respondent No. 2 raised
   a demand of Rs. 45,32,000/- (Rupees Forty Five Lakhs Thirty Two Thousand Only)
   under Section 143(3) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, allegedly on account of
   disallowance of certain expenses.

3. The Petitioner respectfully submits that the said Assessment Order is arbitrary,
   illegal and contrary to the provisions of the Income Tax Act, 1961 and the
   principles of natural justice, in as much as:

   (a) No proper opportunity of being heard was granted to the Petitioner before
       passing the impugned order, in violation of Section 142(1) of the Act.

   (b) The Respondent No. 2 failed to consider the documentary evidence filed
       by the Petitioner vide its letter dated 10th February 2025 (Annexure A).

   (c) The disallowance made is contrary to the settled position of law laid
       down by the Hon'ble Supreme Court in CIT v. Reliance Industries Ltd.
       (2019) 410 ITR 468.

4. The Petitioner has suffered and continues to suffer irreparable loss and injury
   on account of the impugned order and the consequential coercive action threatened
   by the Respondents.

5. The Petitioner has no other equally efficacious remedy available in law.
